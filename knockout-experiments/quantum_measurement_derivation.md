# Quantum Measurement Impossibility via the Universal Explanation Framework

## Derivation: eta = dim(V^G)/dim(V) Predicts Accessible Quantum State Properties

### 1. Setup: Quantum Measurement as an Explanation System

We cast quantum measurement in the language of the Universal Explanation
Impossibility framework by defining an ExplanationSystem:

| Component | Definition |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Theta** (parameter space) | Density matrices rho on C^d (the set of valid quantum states) |
| **Y** (observation space) | Probability vectors (Tr(rho M_i))_i for POVM {M_i} (measurement outcomes) |
| **H** (explanation space) | Bloch vector representation of rho. For d=2: a vector in R^3. For general d: a vector in R^{d^2 - 1} |
| **observe(rho)** | Born rule probabilities: p_i = Tr(rho M_i) |
| **explain(rho)** | The full Bloch vector of rho |
| **incompatible(h1, h2)** | h1 != h2 (any two different states are distinguishable in principle) |

### 2. The Rashomon Property

The Rashomon property holds if and only if the measurement is **informationally
incomplete**: there exist rho_1 != rho_2 with

    Tr(rho_1 M_i) = Tr(rho_2 M_i)  for all i.

This is equivalent to requiring that the POVM {M_i} does not span the full
space of traceless Hermitian operators on C^d. Put differently, the measurement
operators live in a proper subspace of the d^2 - 1 dimensional space of
observables, and distinct states that differ only in the orthogonal complement
of that subspace are observationally indistinguishable.

### 3. Symmetry Group

For a measurement POVM {M_i} with symmetry group G -- the group of unitaries U
such that U M_i U^dagger = M_{pi(i)} for some permutation pi of outcomes -- the
G-action on the Bloch vector space V = R^{d^2-1} is the adjoint representation:

    rho -> U rho U^dagger

induces a real orthogonal transformation on the Bloch vector.

### 4. The eta Formula

The G-invariant subspace V^G consists of Bloch vector components that are
unchanged under all symmetries of the measurement. These are exactly the state
properties that the measurement can determine.

**Claim.** For informationally incomplete measurements with symmetry group G:

    eta = dim(V^G) / dim(V) = (fraction of state properties accessible from measurement)

**Proof sketch.** A Bloch vector component v lies in V^G if and only if
g . v = v for all g in G. By Schur's lemma, the projection onto V^G extracts
exactly those components of rho that are not mixed with other components under
the measurement symmetry. Since the measurement outcome probabilities
p_i = Tr(rho M_i) depend only on the projection of the Bloch vector onto the
span of {M_i}, and the symmetry group G rotates the orthogonal complement among
itself, the measurable information is precisely V^G.

The fraction of total state parameters that are measurable is therefore
dim(V^G) / dim(V).

### 5. Concrete Examples

#### Example 1: Single Pauli-Z measurement on one qubit

- d = 2, so V = R^3 (the Bloch sphere, with components x, y, z).
- POVM: {|0><0|, |1><1|} (projective measurement in the Z-basis).
- Symmetry group: U(1) rotations about the Z-axis, generated by e^{i theta sigma_z / 2}.
- Action on Bloch vector: (x, y, z) -> (x cos theta - y sin theta, x sin theta + y cos theta, z).
- V^G = span{e_z}: only the z-component is invariant under all Z-rotations.
- dim(V^G) = 1, dim(V) = 3.

**eta = 1/3.**

Verification: full qubit tomography requires all three Pauli bases (X, Y, Z),
each providing one independent parameter. A single basis provides 1/3 of the
total state information.

#### Example 2: Pauli-X and Pauli-Z measurements on one qubit

- Combined POVM from both X and Z measurements: {|0><0|, |1><1|, |+><+|, |-><-|}.
- Symmetry: no non-trivial unitary simultaneously preserves both the X and Z
  eigenbases (any such U must commute with both sigma_x and sigma_z, but
  [sigma_x, sigma_z] != 0), so G = {I} restricted to the x-z plane.
- More precisely: measuring X determines the x-component and measuring Z
  determines the z-component. The y-component remains inaccessible.
- V^G = span{e_x, e_z}, dim(V^G) = 2.

**eta = 2/3.**

Two of three Pauli bases access 2/3 of the state information.

#### Example 3: Full Pauli tomography (X, Y, Z) on one qubit

- All three Pauli bases measured.
- G = {I} (trivial -- no non-trivial symmetry).
- V^G = V = R^3.

**eta = 1.** Informationally complete.

#### Example 4: Computational basis measurement on n qubits

- d = 2^n, so V = R^{4^n - 1}.
- POVM: computational basis measurement, with 2^n outcomes.
- The measurement determines only the diagonal of rho in the computational
  basis, i.e., the 2^n - 1 independent populations (probabilities minus
  the trace constraint).
- Symmetry group: includes individual Z-rotations on each qubit (which mix
  the off-diagonal coherence terms among themselves while preserving the diagonal).
- dim(V^G) = 2^n - 1 (the diagonal degrees of freedom).
- dim(V) = 4^n - 1 (all degrees of freedom).

**eta = (2^n - 1) / (4^n - 1).**

| n qubits | d = 2^n | eta = (d-1)/(d^2-1) = 1/(d+1) | Numerical |
|----------|---------|-------------------------------|-----------|
| 1 | 2 | 1/3 | 0.333 |
| 2 | 4 | 1/5 | 0.200 |
| 3 | 8 | 1/9 | 0.111 |
| 4 | 16 | 1/17 | 0.059 |
| 10 | 1024 | 1/1025 | 0.00098 |

For large n, eta ~ 1/2^n: an exponentially small fraction of the quantum state
is accessible from the computational basis alone. This is the well-known fact
that computational basis measurement reveals only populations, missing all
coherences.

### 6. Connection to Known Results

#### Classical shadow tomography (Huang, Kueng, Preskill 2020)

The sample complexity for estimating Tr(rho O) using random Pauli measurements
is governed by the "shadow norm" of O. For k-local observables, this scales as
O(3^k). The framework prediction: random Pauli measurements break most
symmetries (G is small), so eta is close to 1 and most properties are
accessible with enough samples. This is consistent with the efficiency of
classical shadow tomography.

#### Holevo bound

The classical capacity of a measurement channel Phi(rho) = diag(rho) in the
measurement basis is bounded by the Holevo quantity:

    chi(Phi) = max [ S(sum_i p_i Phi(rho_i)) - sum_i p_i S(Phi(rho_i)) ]

For computational basis measurement, the eta formula gives:

    eta = (d-1)/(d^2-1) = 1/(d+1)

The Holevo capacity per symbol is log(d), while the total state information
scales as log(d^2) = 2 log(d), giving a ratio of 1/2. Comparing:

| d | eta = 1/(d+1) | Holevo ratio = 1/2 |
|---|---------------|-------------------|
| 2 | 0.333 | 0.500 |
| 4 | 0.200 | 0.500 |
| 8 | 0.111 | 0.500 |

The eta formula and Holevo ratio measure different things: eta counts the
fraction of Bloch vector components (a geometric measure), while the Holevo
ratio counts bits (an entropic measure). The gap is expected and documented.
For d=2, eta = 1/3 while the Holevo ratio is 1/2, reflecting that information
content per parameter is not uniform across the Bloch sphere.

### 8. Formal Statement

**Theorem (Quantum Measurement Specialization).** Let {M_i} be a POVM on C^d
with symmetry group G acting on the Bloch vector space V = R^{d^2-1} via the
adjoint representation. Then:

(a) The Rashomon property holds if and only if span{M_i} is a proper subspace
    of the traceless Hermitian operators on C^d.

(b) When the Rashomon property holds:

        eta = dim(V^G) / dim(V)

    equals the fraction of independent quantum state parameters determinable
    from the measurement.

(c) For computational basis measurement on d-dimensional systems:

        eta = (d - 1) / (d^2 - 1) = 1 / (d + 1)

    recovering the known population-to-coherence ratio.
