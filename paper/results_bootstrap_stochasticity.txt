INTERPRETATION
============================================================
DATA-LEVEL STOCHASTICITY ALONE PRODUCES COMPARABLE INSTABILITY.

With subsample=1.0 and a fixed XGBoost random_state=42, the only source of variation is which 80% of training rows each model sees. This setting produced 50/50 distinct rankings, max flip rate 0.5094 (vs 0.4702 for XGBoost subsampling), and mean Kendall tau distance 0.459498 (vs 0.393291 for XGBoost subsampling). 

RESPONSE TO REVIEWER R2: The instability is NOT an artifact of XGBoost's internal subsample= parameter. It arises from the Rashomon property — under collinearity there exist many near-equivalent models that disagree on feature rankings. Any source of stochasticity (internal subsampling, bootstrap resampling, data drift, etc.) suffices to traverse the Rashomon set and reveal instability. The theoretical impossibility result holds regardless of how the Rashomon set is traversed.
