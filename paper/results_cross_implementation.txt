Notes:
  F1 correlation: Pearson r between clip(|mean_diff|/SE, 0, 20) and flip rate
  F5 precision:   TP/(TP+FP) where TP = flagged (Z_F5>1.96) correlated pairs
                  that are truly unstable (flip>10%); FP = flagged but stable
  DASH flip rate: max per-pair flip rate within M=25 ensemble (corr. pairs only)

CatBoost F5 note:
  CatBoost's get_feature_importance() returns gain-based importance (not split
  counts), producing a different Z-statistic distribution than XGBoost/LightGBM
  weight-based counts. The F5 diagnostic as formulated (split-frequency Z) does
  not transfer cleanly to CatBoost; F5 precision = 0.000 means no correlated
  pair was flagged at Z>1.96 with gain importance. This is a known limitation of
  the F5 diagnostic for gain-based importances, not a failure of the underlying
  instability — CatBoost still shows 160 unstable pairs and max flip rate 0.500.

Interpretation:
  All three implementations exhibit similar instability levels, confirming
  the impossibility is a mathematical property of collinear features under
  the Rashomon effect — not an artifact of any particular GBDT implementation.
