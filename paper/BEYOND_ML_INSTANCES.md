# Beyond-ML Instances of the Universal Explanation Impossibility

Reference catalog of domains where the Rashomon property holds and the
impossibility theorem applies. The first 8 are covered in the current paper
(§7.5). The rest are candidates for a follow-up "Impossibility Across Science"
paper.

---

## In the paper (8 domains)

### 1. Philosophy of science (Quine–Duhem)
- **Rashomon property**: Empirical evidence underdetermines theory choice —
  multiple theories are empirically adequate.
- **Resolution**: Structural realism (stable content = what's invariant across
  equivalent theories). Dropping decisiveness = constructive empiricism
  (van Fraassen).
- **References**: Quine 1951, Duhem 1906, Laudan 1991, Worrall 1989,
  van Fraassen 1980, Okasha 2011.

### 2. Neuroscience (degeneracy)
- **Rashomon property**: Crustacean stomatogastric ganglia producing identical
  motor patterns are built from qualitatively different ionic conductance
  profiles (Marder & Goaillard 2006). Edelman & Gally (2001) formalized as
  "degeneracy."
- **Resolution**: Report the class of circuits consistent with observed behavior,
  not a single circuit.
- **References**: Marder & Goaillard 2006, Edelman & Gally 2001.

### 3. Economics (partial identification)
- **Rashomon property**: Observational data don't pin down a unique model; the
  identified set is the observe-fiber.
- **Resolution**: Report bounds on the identified set rather than a point
  estimate.
- **References**: Manski 2003.

### 4. Medical diagnosis
- **Rashomon property**: Multiple conditions produce the same symptoms.
- **Resolution**: Differential diagnosis — list all conditions consistent with
  symptoms. Sacrifices decisiveness for faithfulness + stability.

### 5. Physics — gauge invariance
- **Rashomon property**: Infinitely many gauge potentials A_μ produce the same
  field tensor F_μν. The gauge group acts transitively on each fiber.
- **Resolution**: Gauge fixing (Coulomb, Lorenz, axial) = G-invariant
  resolution. Physics arrived at this a century before ML.
- **References**: Yang & Mills 1954, Faddeev & Popov 1967.

### 6. Psychometrics — factor indeterminacy
- **Rashomon property**: Factor loading matrix identified only up to rotation;
  infinitely many rotated solutions explain the same covariance structure.
- **Resolution**: Varimax / promax rotation = G-invariant resolution (select
  rotation satisfying a symmetry condition). Thurstone was grappling with the
  trilemma in 1947.
- **References**: Thurstone 1947, Kaiser 1958.

### 7. Climate / hydrology — equifinality
- **Rashomon property**: Many parameter configurations produce acceptably similar
  fits to observed data (Beven's "equifinality").
- **Resolution**: GLUE — report the full set of behavioral parameter sets rather
  than a single best-fit model.
- **References**: Beven & Binley 1993.

### 8. Social choice (Arrow) — analogy, not instance
- **Structure**: IIA + Pareto + non-dictatorship → impossible. Same trilemma
  shape but complementary mechanism: Arrow's IIA *prevents* Rashomon rather
  than being generated by it.
- **Note**: Treated as analogy in the paper, not a formal instance.
- **References**: Arrow 1951, Nipkow 2009 (Isabelle/HOL formalization).

## Candidates for follow-up work

### 9. Statistical mechanics — microstate degeneracy
- **Θ**: Microstates (particle positions and momenta).
- **Y**: Macrostates (temperature, pressure, volume).
- **H**: Mechanistic explanation of why the system has this macrostate.
- **Rashomon property**: Astronomically many microstates produce the same
  macrostate (Boltzmann). The explanation "which microstate are we in?"
  is radically underdetermined.
- **Resolution**: Thermodynamic potentials (free energy, entropy) are invariant
  under microstate permutations = G-invariant resolution.
- **Strength**: Extremely clean instance. The symmetry group is the permutation
  group on indistinguishable particles.

### 10. Evolutionary biology — convergent evolution
- **Θ**: Evolutionary pathways (genetic configurations + selection history).
- **Y**: Phenotype (observable trait).
- **H**: Evolutionary explanation of how the trait arose.
- **Rashomon property**: Wings evolved independently in birds, bats, and insects.
  Camera eyes evolved independently in vertebrates and cephalopods. Different
  genetic configurations → same phenotypic observable.
- **Resolution**: Bayesian posterior over phylogenetic trees — report the
  distribution, not a single tree.
- **Strength**: Well-documented. The "convergent evolution" literature is vast.

### 11. Protein science — sequence degeneracy
- **Θ**: Amino acid sequences.
- **Y**: 3D protein structure.
- **H**: Explanation of why the protein has this fold.
- **Rashomon property**: Many sequences fold to the same structure. The sequence
  → structure map is many-to-one.
- **Resolution**: Sequence logos, position-specific scoring matrices (PSSMs) —
  report the distribution over compatible sequences.
- **Strength**: Well-established in structural biology.

### 12. Chemistry — reaction mechanism underdetermination
- **Θ**: Mechanistic pathways (elementary steps, intermediates, transition states).
- **Y**: Observable kinetics (rate law, product distribution).
- **H**: Mechanistic explanation of how products form.
- **Rashomon property**: Multiple mechanisms can produce the same rate law and
  product distribution. The Curtin–Hammett principle is a special case: when
  intermediates equilibrate fast, the product ratio is independent of starting
  conditions, but the actual pathway differs.
- **Resolution**: Report all mechanisms consistent with the kinetic data; design
  experiments to distinguish them (isotope labeling, trapping intermediates).
- **Strength**: Textbook example in organic chemistry.

### 13. Seismology / geophysics — inverse problems
- **Θ**: Subsurface structure (velocity, density distribution).
- **Y**: Surface seismic measurements (seismograms).
- **H**: Explanation of subsurface structure.
- **Rashomon property**: Multiple subsurface configurations produce the same
  surface measurements. Non-uniqueness is fundamental to inverse problems.
- **Resolution**: Tomographic inversions with uncertainty quantification — report
  bounds or posterior distributions, not a single subsurface model.
- **References**: Tarantola 2005 (Inverse Problem Theory).

### 14. Linguistics — structural ambiguity
- **Θ**: Parse trees / syntactic derivations.
- **Y**: Surface string (the sentence as heard/read).
- **H**: Structural explanation (which parse tree is intended).
- **Rashomon property**: "I saw the man with the telescope" has two parses
  (instrument vs. accompaniment). Multiple derivations produce the same string.
- **Resolution**: Packed parse forests that represent all parses simultaneously;
  probabilistic parsing that reports distributions over trees.
- **Quine variant**: Quine's indeterminacy of translation is an even stronger
  version — multiple translation manuals compatible with all behavioral evidence,
  with no fact of the matter about correctness.

### 15. Quantum mechanics — state underdetermination
- **Θ**: Quantum states (density matrices / pure states).
- **Y**: Measurement statistics (Born rule probabilities).
- **H**: Explanation of the quantum state.
- **Rashomon property**: Multiple density matrices can produce the same
  measurement statistics for a given observable set. Purifications of a mixed
  state are observationally equivalent but structurally different.
- **Resolution**: Report the equivalence class of states consistent with
  measurements (quantum state tomography with uncertainty).
- **Meta-level variant**: Copenhagen vs. Many-Worlds vs. Bohm — same predictions,
  incompatible ontological explanations. Whether "incompatible" is well-defined
  here is philosophically contentious.
- **Strength**: Very clean for the density matrix version. The meta-interpretation
  version is suggestive but harder to formalize.

### 16. Cryptography — preimage multiplicity
- **Θ**: Inputs (preimages).
- **Y**: Hash outputs.
- **H**: Explanation of which input produced this hash.
- **Rashomon property**: Hash functions are many-to-one by design. Multiple
  inputs produce the same hash.
- **Resolution**: Report the preimage set (infeasible by design) or accept
  underdetermination.
- **Note**: Somewhat trivial as an instance — the Rashomon property is by
  construction, not emergent.

### 17. Database theory — query equivalence
- **Θ**: Query execution plans.
- **Y**: Result sets.
- **H**: Explanation of why this plan was chosen.
- **Rashomon property**: Multiple plans produce identical results with different
  execution strategies.
- **Resolution**: Query optimizer reports cost estimates for multiple plans
  rather than committing to one without justification.

### 18. Legal interpretation
- **Θ**: Legal theories / doctrinal frameworks.
- **Y**: Case holdings (outcomes).
- **H**: Legal explanation (which principle justifies the holding).
- **Rashomon property**: Multiple legal theories justify the same holding
  (e.g., originalism vs. living constitutionalism reaching the same result).
- **Resolution**: Report the range of supporting doctrines; acknowledge
  underdetermination in judicial reasoning.
- **Strength**: Interesting but formalism may be too loose for rigorous
  instantiation. Better as suggestive remark.

### 19. General relativity — coordinate freedom
- **Θ**: Coordinate representations of spacetime.
- **Y**: Physical observables (proper time, geodesic deviation, curvature
  invariants).
- **H**: Coordinate-dependent description of "what's happening."
- **Rashomon property**: Diffeomorphism invariance — infinitely many coordinate
  systems describe the same physical geometry.
- **Resolution**: Use coordinate-invariant quantities (curvature tensors,
  scalar invariants) = G-invariant resolution where G is the diffeomorphism
  group.
- **Strength**: Extremely clean. Einstein's hole argument is essentially about
  the Rashomon property for spacetime.

## What's importantly NOT an instance

- **Arrow's theorem**: Complementary, not instance. IIA prevents Rashomon rather
  than being generated by it.
- **Gödel's incompleteness**: About provability limits, not about multiple
  configurations producing the same observable.
- **The halting problem**: Decidability of a single question, not explanation
  under equivalence.
- **No-free-lunch theorems**: About learning, not explaining a fixed system.
- **Heisenberg uncertainty**: About measurement disturbance, not about multiple
  configurations producing the same observations. (But density matrix QM IS an
  instance — see #15 above.)

## Strategy for follow-up paper

See `RESEARCH_ROADMAP.md` (repo root) for the full venue strategy, timeline,
and sequencing of all papers. The cross-science paper draft is at
`paper/cross_science_impossibility_draft.tex`.

**Venue**: PNAS first, then Science if needed. See roadmap for full ranking.

**Title**: "One Theorem, Twelve Names: A Universal Law of Explanation Across Science"

**Draft**: `paper/cross_science_impossibility_draft.tex`

**Core contribution**: Formally instantiate the theorem in 7+ non-ML domains.
Show that each community independently discovered the Rashomon property (under
different names: gauge freedom, degeneracy, equifinality, factor indeterminacy,
Markov equivalence, partial identification) and converged on structurally
identical resolutions (gauge fixing, circuit-class reports, GLUE, varimax,
CPDAGs, identified set bounds).

**Strongest candidates for formal treatment** (in order):
1. Gauge invariance (physics) — cleanest, most established
2. Statistical mechanics — enormous fiber, clear resolution
3. Factor indeterminacy (psychometrics) — well-formalized, rotation group
4. Convergent evolution (biology) — well-documented, phylogenetic posteriors
5. General relativity (coordinate freedom) — diffeomorphism group, clean math
6. Reaction mechanisms (chemistry) — Curtin–Hammett, textbook example
