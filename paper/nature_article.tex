\begin{abstract}
We prove that no explanation of an underspecified system can simultaneously
be faithful (consistent with internal structure), stable (invariant across
equivalent configurations), and decisive (committing to every internal
distinction).  This impossibility is derived from first principles in eight
scientific domains---each requiring zero shared axioms---and verified in the
Lean~4 proof assistant (104~files, 463~theorems, 0~unproved goals).
Empirical validation in seven domains confirms the predicted dose-response
between underspecification and instability.  The constructive
resolution---$G$-invariant projection---is Pareto-optimal, unifying
century-old domain-specific practices (gauge-invariant observables,
CPDAGs, minimum-norm solutions) as instances of a single strategy.
For maximally incompatible systems, the impossibility strengthens to a
bilemma: even faithful~+~stable requires enriching the explanation space.
Three quantitative predictions---an invariance counting law, a Gaussian
flip rate formula, and a universal instability predictor ($R^2 = 0.957$
across seven domains)---are confirmed by experiment.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Introduction}

The codons UCU and UCC both encode serine: same protein fragment, different
nucleotide sequences~\citep{crick1966codon}.  A causal chain $A \to B \to C$
and a fork $A \leftarrow B \to C$ encode the same conditional
independences~\citep{verma1991equivalence}.  Two gauge configurations can
share the same holonomy while differing in local edge
labels~\citep{jackson1999}.  In each case, the observable output does not
uniquely determine the internal structure that produced it---a structural
feature of underspecified inference present whenever the mapping from
configuration to observation is many-to-one.

Practitioners in each field have independently developed coping
strategies---gauge-invariant observables, CPDAGs, Patterson
maps~\citep{hauptman1953solution}, codon usage
tables~\citep{crick1966codon}---each involving projection onto the
subspace invariant under the symmetry group of equivalent configurations,
yet no framework has connected them or proved their optimality.

Here we prove a universal impossibility: when a system has the
\emph{Rashomon property}---two configurations with the same observable but
incompatible internal structures---no explanation can be simultaneously
faithful, stable, and decisive (Theorem~\ref{thm:impossibility}).  We
derive this in eight scientific domains, each requiring zero shared axioms,
and verify the framework in Lean~4~\citep{demoura2021lean4} (104~files,
463~theorems, 0~unproved goals).  Empirical validation in seven domains
confirms the predicted dose-response (Table~\ref{tab:unification},
Fig.~\ref{fig:dose-response}).  The $G$-invariant resolution is
Pareto-optimal, explaining why eight communities converged independently on
the same strategy.

\section*{Results}

\subsection*{The impossibility}

An explanation is \emph{faithful} if it never contradicts the system's
internal report; \emph{stable} if observationally equivalent configurations
receive the same explanation; and \emph{decisive} if it commits to every
distinction the internal structure makes.  Formal definitions are in Methods.

\begin{theorem}[The explanation impossibility]
\label{thm:impossibility}
If a system has the Rashomon property---there exist $\theta_1, \theta_2$
with $\mathrm{obs}(\theta_1) = \mathrm{obs}(\theta_2)$ and
$\mathrm{exp}(\theta_1) \incomp \mathrm{exp}(\theta_2)$---then no
explanation map $E$ is simultaneously faithful, stable, and decisive.
\end{theorem}

The proof is a four-step contradiction chain (Methods).  The impossibility
is tight: each pair of properties is achievable via Lean-verified witnesses
(Fig.~\ref{fig:trilemma}), and a biconditional shows impossibility holds
if and only if Rashomon is present.

\subsection*{Three quantitative predictions}

The framework generates testable predictions beyond classification.

\paragraph{Invariance counting (cf.\ Noether's theorem).}
The invariance correspondence makes a sharp quantitative prediction:
for $P$~features arranged in $g$~correlation groups, only $\binom{g}{2}$
independent ranking facts are stably answerable.  We tested this with
$P = 12$ features in $g = 3$ groups (200~models, both Ridge and XGBoost).
The result is a clean bimodal distribution: all 18~within-group pairs show
${\sim}50\%$ flip rate, all 48~between-group pairs show $< 2\%$ flip
rate---a 47-percentage-point gap (permutation $p < 0.0002$; Cohen's
$d = 1.41$).  A sensitivity analysis across $\rho \in [0.50, 0.99]$
confirms the gap is \emph{invariant} across the entire correlation range.

\paragraph{Gaussian flip rate.}
On real data where feature importance is approximately (not exactly) equal
within groups, $\text{flip}(j,k) =
2\Phi(\Delta/\sigma)\Phi(-\Delta/\sigma)$ predicts per-pair flip rates
with out-of-sample $R^2 = 0.848$ (mean across five clinical and financial
datasets, using independent calibration and validation model sets).  The
formula is standard signal detection theory; the framework's contribution
is the structural identification of \emph{which} pairs are subject to
this formula (derivation in Methods).  Pairs with $|\Delta|/\sigma > 2$ are
reliable (flip rate $< 5\%$); pairs with $|\Delta|/\sigma < 0.5$ are
unreliable (flip rate $> 30\%$).
On gene expression data (10{,}935 genes, cancer classification), 70\% of gene
importance comparisons are unreliable using gain-based importance (42\% with TreeSHAP; $R^2 = 0.51$).
Across seven real clinical, financial, and genomics datasets (2{,}062
feature-pair comparisons), 62\% are unreliable and only 4\% are reliable
(62\% of 2{,}062 feature-pair comparisons across seven clinical, financial,
and genomics datasets; 49\% across the broader 22{,}332-pair audit
including synthetic data).

\paragraph{Universal $\eta$ law.}
The character-theoretic formula $\eta = \dim(V^G)/\dim(V)$
(the fraction of explanation content that survives averaging over equivalent
configurations)---a standard
ratio in representation theory whose application to explanation instability
is new---predicts the observed instability rate across seven
well-characterized domain instances with $R^2 = 0.957$, slope~$= 0.91$,
and intercept~$= 0.008$ ($p = 1.4 \times 10^{-4}$)---a parameter-free
predictor spanning ML attribution, concept probes, model selection, codon
degeneracy, and statistical mechanics.

\subsection*{Cross-domain validation}

We derive the impossibility as a zero-axiom consequence in eight scientific
domains (Table~\ref{tab:unification}), each constructively witnessing the
Rashomon property from domain-specific first principles alone.  The eight
instances involve fundamentally different symmetry groups
(Table~\ref{tab:structural}), spanning continuous ($\R^{n-r}$), finite
permutation ($S_k$), and ambiguity ($\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_n
\times \mathbb{Z}_2$) groups.

The predicted dose-response---more underspecification produces more
instability---is confirmed in all seven empirically tested domains
(Fig.~\ref{fig:dose-response}), each with negative controls confirming
stability when Rashomon is absent (combined binomial $p = 0.008$;
individual $p$-values from $10^{-38}$ to $10^{-3}$).  Per-domain
statistics are in Methods and Supporting Information.

\subsection*{The bilemma}

For \emph{maximally incompatible} systems---where the only compatible pair
is $(h,h)$, as in binary rankings and DAG orientations---the trilemma
strengthens.

\begin{theorem}[The Bilemma]
\label{thm:bilemma}
For any maximally incompatible system with the Rashomon property, no
explanation is simultaneously faithful and stable.
\end{theorem}

\noindent Tightness collapses from 3/3 achievable pairs to 1/3 (proof in Methods):

\section*{Discussion}

The theorem reframes ``why are explanations unstable?''\ as ``because the
inference problem is underspecified.''  Practitioners can diagnose whether
instability is structural (Rashomon holds; no method eliminates it) or
methodological (Rashomon absent; fixable).  The query-relative version
identifies which questions are stably answerable: a biologist can stably
report the amino acid but not the codon; a statistician can stably report
the CPDAG but not the DAG.

Nine ML instances (Supporting Information) instantiate the theorem for
feature attribution, attention, counterfactuals, concept probes, causal
discovery, model selection, saliency maps, token citation, and circuit
discovery---findings relevant to the EU AI Act (Article~13) and FDA's
AI/ML Action Plan~\citep{fda2021aiml,selbst2018intuitive}.  In Bayesian
terms, faithfulness is likelihood consistency, stability is posterior
invariance, and the $G$-invariant resolution is the posterior predictive;
Pareto-optimality follows from Bayes admissibility~\citep{berger1985statistical,halmos1949application}.
The result parallels Arrow's impossibility~\citep{arrow1951social}---both
show symmetry forces trade-offs---but is constructive: it identifies the
unique optimal trade-off and proves existing practice implements it.
Individual domain instances are separately known; the contribution is the
meta-theorem unifying them under a single structural condition and producing
three novel quantitative predictions.

\paragraph{Cross-domain transfer.}
Applying DASH to causal discovery reduces flip rate fourfold
($p < 10^{-10}$) at reduced decisiveness (0.21 vs.\ 0.36).
The character-theoretic prediction that information loss scales as $1/k$
for $S_k$ predicts protein sequence diversity ranking (Spearman $\rho =
0.88$, $p = 0.004$).

\paragraph{Limitations.}
Derivations use minimal two-element witnesses; empirical validations use
domain-appropriate but not exhaustive datasets.  The resolution assumes
known equivalence classes.  The theorem addresses existence, not
computational cost.  Three conjectured extensions were pre-registered and
falsified, delineating the framework's boundary (Supporting Information).

\paragraph{Scientific revolutions.} The bilemma pattern---maximally
incompatible explanation space, enrichment necessary, decisiveness
sacrificed---characterizes the structure of paradigm shifts that preserve
the configuration space. The transition
from classical to quantum mechanics ($H = \{\text{definite value},
\text{superposition}\}$, enrichment = ``no fact of the matter'') and from
Newtonian to relativistic physics ($H = \{\text{simultaneous},
\text{not simultaneous}\}$, enrichment = spacetime) both follow the
pattern, with the bilemma Lean-verified for each
(Table~\ref{tab:revolutions}).
Not all resolutions are enrichment events: Bohmian mechanics resolves the
measurement problem by changing the configuration space (adding particle
positions), which is a system change outside the bilemma's scope.
Whether paradigm shifts \emph{in general}
contain an enrichment component is a conjecture supported by five formalized
instances.
The framework extends to AI value alignment: when multiple RLHF runs produce equally well-performing models with different implicit value priorities, the bilemma proves that no value explanation is simultaneously faithful and stable.

\section*{Methods}

\paragraph{Formal framework.}
An \emph{explanation system} is a tuple $\mathcal{S} = (\Params, \sH, Y,
\mathsf{obs}, \mathsf{exp}, \incomp)$ where $\Params$ is a configuration
space, $\sH$ is an explanation space, $Y$ is an observable space,
$\mathsf{obs} : \Params \to Y$ maps configurations to observables,
$\mathsf{exp} : \Params \to \sH$ maps configurations to their internal
explanations, and $\incomp$ is an incompatibility relation on $\sH$.
The system has the \emph{Rashomon property} if there exist
$\theta_1, \theta_2 \in \Params$ with $\mathsf{obs}(\theta_1) =
\mathsf{obs}(\theta_2)$ and $\mathsf{exp}(\theta_1) \incomp
\mathsf{exp}(\theta_2)$.

An explanation map $E : \Params \to \sH$ is \emph{faithful} if
$\lnot(E(\theta) \incomp \mathsf{exp}(\theta))$ for all $\theta$;
\emph{stable} if $\mathsf{obs}(\theta_1) = \mathsf{obs}(\theta_2)$ implies
$E(\theta_1) = E(\theta_2)$; and \emph{decisive} if, for all $\theta$ and
$h \in \sH$, $\mathsf{exp}(\theta) \incomp h$ implies $E(\theta) \incomp h$.

\paragraph{Lean~4 formalization.}
Built on Lean~4 v4.30.0-rc1~\citep{demoura2021lean4} with
Mathlib~\citep{mathlib2020}.  The core \texttt{ExplanationSystem} type is
parametric in $\Params$, $\sH$, $Y$.  Each derived instance constructs a
Rashomon witness via \texttt{decide} (zero axioms).  Compilation:
\texttt{lake build} ($\sim$5~min).

\paragraph{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:impossibility}.}
Suppose $E$ is faithful, stable, and decisive.  Let $\theta_1, \theta_2$
witness the Rashomon property.
(1)~Decisiveness at $\theta_1$: $E(\theta_1) \incomp \mathsf{exp}(\theta_2)$.
(2)~Stability: $E(\theta_1) = E(\theta_2)$, so $E(\theta_2) \incomp
\mathsf{exp}(\theta_2)$.
(3)~Faithfulness at $\theta_2$:
$\lnot(E(\theta_2) \incomp \mathsf{exp}(\theta_2))$.
Contradiction.  All three properties are load-bearing; a weaker pairwise
decisiveness variant makes faithfulness superfluous (Supporting Information).

\paragraph{Resolution framework.}
The $G$-invariant resolution is formalized as follows.  Given a group $G$
acting on $\Params$ such that $g \cdot \theta$ preserves observables
($\mathsf{obs}(g \cdot \theta) = \mathsf{obs}(\theta)$ for all $g \in G$),
an explanation map $E$ is \emph{$G$-invariant} if $E(g \cdot \theta) =
E(\theta)$ for all $g$.  We prove: (a)~every $G$-invariant map is stable;
(b)~the orbit-averaged map $\bar{E}(\theta) = |G|^{-1} \sum_{g \in G}
E(g \cdot \theta)$ is $G$-invariant (for finite groups; the integral
analogue holds for compact groups); (c)~$\bar{E}$ is Pareto-optimal among
stable maps with respect to pointwise faithfulness.

\paragraph{Bilemma proof.}
A system is \emph{maximally incompatible} if $\lnot\mathrm{incompatible}(h_1,h_2) \Rightarrow h_1 = h_2$.
Faithfulness then forces $E = \mathsf{exp}$, which is decisive; the
trilemma gives the contradiction.  ML instances (SHAP sign, feature
selection, counterfactual direction) trigger the bilemma.

\paragraph{Gaussian flip rate derivation.}
For features $j,k$ with importance difference $d^{(m)} \sim N(\Delta,
\sigma^2)$, the flip probability is $\text{flip}(j,k) =
2\Phi(\Delta/\sigma)\Phi(-\Delta/\sigma)$.  Validation uses independent
calibration and validation model sets (30 each) on five datasets.

\paragraph{Derived instances.}
Each of the eight instances defines an \texttt{ExplanationSystem} with
domain-specific types and minimal (two-element) Rashomon witnesses.
The attribution instance generalizes \citet{bilodeau2024impossibility} from
Shapley axioms to the abstract framework; the causal instance derives the
Rashomon property from Markov equivalence~\citep{verma1991equivalence}.

\paragraph{Empirical validation.}
Seven domains are validated with dose-response designs: underspecification
is varied and instability measured, with negative controls confirming
stability when Rashomon is absent.
\emph{Mathematics:} $Ax=b$ with four solvers, null-space dimension
$1$--$10$ (Mann--Whitney $U$).
\emph{Biology:} codon entropy across 120 cytochrome~c sequences grouped
by degeneracy (Kruskal--Wallis).
\emph{Gauge theory:} $\mathbb{Z}_2$ edge-label orbit variance on
triangle graphs.
\emph{Linguistics:} four parsers on 50 ambiguous/50 unambiguous sentences
(Wilcoxon).
\emph{Crystallography:} phase retrieval RMSD with/without positivity
constraints (Mann--Whitney $U$).
\emph{Computer science:} census disaggregation KL divergence vs.\ county
count (Spearman $\rho$).
\emph{Statistics:} causal discovery (PC, GES) on Asia network,
100~seeds (Mann--Whitney $U$).
All tests use $\alpha = 0.05$, non-parametric statistics, and fixed
seeds.  No multiple-comparison correction across domains; binomial
$p = 0.008$ provides combined assessment.

\paragraph{Reproducibility.}
All experiment scripts, data processing code, and figure generation code
are provided in the repository.  The complete experimental pipeline can be
reproduced with \texttt{make validate} ($\sim$5~min).  The Lean
formalization can be rebuilt with \texttt{lake build} ($\sim$5~min).
All paper versions can be compiled with \texttt{make paper}.  The biology
experiment requires an internet connection (NCBI Entrez API) and
BioPython; all other experiments are self-contained.
